Types of Business Loan

Business Loan

A general loan for businesses to manage operations, expansion, or unforeseen expenses. It can be secured (requires collateral) or unsecured (no collateral required).

Equipment Loan

Helps businesses purchase machinery, vehicles, or equipment needed for operations. The equipment itself serves as collateral, reducing risk for lenders.

Working Capital Loan

Short-term financing to cover daily operational expenses like salaries, inventory, and rent. Ideal for businesses facing seasonal demand fluctuations.

Government Loan

Loans offered under government schemes to support startups and small businesses. Examples include MUDRA loans, Stand-Up India, and CGTMSE loans.

Term Loan

A lump-sum loan repaid in fixed installments over a specified period (short-term or long-term). Used for expansion, infrastructure, or large purchases.

Invoice Financing

Businesses sell their unpaid invoices to lenders in exchange for immediate cash. Helps maintain cash flow while waiting for client payments.

Real Estate Loan

Provides financing for purchasing commercial properties like office spaces, warehouses, or retail shops. The property itself acts as collateral.
